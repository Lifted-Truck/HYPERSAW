The notch positions are the coupled swarm. K positive herds every notch to one frequency — a single deep moving null; negative spreads them into an even comb — the classic phaser, but as an attractor state rather than a preset. Drift (on by default) is the "phaser sweep" reimagined: notches wander as seeded random walks instead of an LFO — no loop, ever. Gravity positive parks notches on the harmonics of the last note — tuned rejection, the note's own partials get eaten; negative forbids notches from touching harmonics — the wander that always misses the note. Feedback sharpens the ridges between notches.
