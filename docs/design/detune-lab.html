Keyboard — Ableton layout · 8-voice poly

Computer keys, Live-style: A S D F G H J K L = white keys from C, W E · T Y U · O P = black keys, Z / X = octave down / up. Hold chords — each note is its own swarm.
Pull K is calibrated in units of the swarm's own frequency spread σ, measured live — so the phase transition sits mid-slider at any detune, law, or register. Sweep slowly through 1.5–2.5×σ while holding a note: free cloud → slow herding → shimmering partial lock → hollow static comb → single loud saw. The collapse into mono-ish width at full lock is the physics, not a bug: coherence is narrowness. K is bipolar: negative pulls each voice toward its own evenly-spaced seat (amber rings) — locked splay of N saws sums toward a saw N× higher, quieter, because splay cancels every harmonic that isn't a multiple of N. Reading the circle: sync = dots clump to a point (R₁→1) · splay = a rigid regular polygon parked on its seats (R₁→0, Rₙ→1) · free = a drifting scribble, both meters low. Inertia gives voices momentum, so the swarm overshoots and rings instead of snapping. R → tone routes sync order to a filter (▼lock = darkens as it locks, ▲lock = opens as it dissolves). Turn retrigger off to watch onset-lock herd a scattered swarm. Drift is a seeded random walk — same seed, same wander.
Spread scales the detune depth from 1 semitone up to two octaves; anchor ↑ slides the whole swarm upward so the lowest voice stays pinned to the root while the rest fan up (NI-style extreme spread). Anchor at root ↑ with spread near 12 st and detune high gives the octave-wide, bottom-locked stack; back anchor to bipolar for the classic symmetric detune.
